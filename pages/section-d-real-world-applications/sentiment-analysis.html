Sentiment Analysis

Prepared jointly by: Nupoor Mahajan & Hansa Gusaiwal

What It Does

Sentiment analysis automatically determines whether a piece of text - like a product review - expresses a positive, negative, or neutral opinion.

Relevant Week 1 Concepts

Text cleaning, stop-word removal (carefully, to avoid removing negations), TF-IDF, and models like Random Forest, XGBoost, or LSTM built on top of these features.

Why Those Concepts Are Needed

Raw review text must first be cleaned and converted into numeric features before any classifier can learn the relationship between word patterns and sentiment labels.

Expected Benefits

Businesses can automatically monitor customer opinions at scale, without manually reading thousands of reviews.

Real-world example In an Amazon reviews sentiment analysis project, reviews are cleaned, converted into TF-IDF or embedding features, and then classified as positive or negative using models like Random Forest and LSTM, tracked with tools like MLflow.
Connection to theory Sentiment analysis is a direct, practical use case of nearly every Week 1 concept - from preprocessing and feature engineering all the way to the choice of language representation.
