Machine Translation

Prepared jointly by: Nupoor Mahajan & Hansa Gusaiwal

What It Does

Machine translation automatically converts text from one language to another - for example, English to Hindi - while trying to preserve meaning.

Relevant Week 1 Concepts

Tokenization (especially tricky for languages without clear word boundaries), sub-word tokenization, and contextual/transformer-based embeddings.

Why Those Concepts Are Needed

Different languages have very different grammar and word structures, so accurate tokenization and rich contextual representation are essential to avoid literal, awkward translations.

Expected Benefits

Breaks language barriers, enabling communication and access to information across different linguistic communities, including India's many regional languages.

Real-world example Translating "The bank is closed today" requires the model to first correctly represent the contextual meaning of "bank" (financial institution, not riverbank) before generating an accurate translation.
Connection to theory Modern machine translation relies heavily on Transformer-based Representations, since word-by-word translation without context often produces incorrect or awkward results.
