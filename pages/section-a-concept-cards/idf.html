Feature Engineering

Inverse Document Frequency (IDF)

Measuring how rare or common a term is across an entire collection of documents, to down-weight overly common words.

feature engineeringstatisticsbuilding block for TF-IDF
Prepared jointly by: Nupoor Mahajan & Hansa Gusaiwal
1 Definition

Inverse Document Frequency (IDF) measures how rare or informative a word is across an entire corpus (collection of documents). Words that appear in many documents get a low IDF, while rare, distinguishing words get a high IDF.

2 Purpose

Some words appear frequently in almost every document (like "product" in a set of product reviews) but do not help distinguish one document's topic from another. IDF reduces the weight of such common words while boosting rarer, more distinguishing ones.

3 Working Principle

IDF for a word w is typically calculated as:

IDF ( w ) = log ( total number of documents number of documents containing w )

Words appearing in nearly every document get an IDF close to zero; rare words get a much higher IDF.

4 Example
Worked example In a corpus of 1,000 reviews, the word "screen" appears in 500 reviews (common → low IDF), while "overheating" appears in only 10 reviews (rare → high IDF, making it a more distinguishing term).
5 Advantages
  • Automatically down-weights generic, overused words.
  • Highlights terms that are more specific and informative for a document.
  • Works alongside TF to build far more effective features than raw counts.
6 Limitations
  • IDF alone, without TF, does not reflect how important a word is within a specific document.
  • Needs the full corpus to compute meaningfully - it cannot be calculated from a single document.
7 Applications
  • Search engine ranking algorithms.
  • Identifying keywords unique to a specific review or article.
  • Document clustering and topic distinction.
