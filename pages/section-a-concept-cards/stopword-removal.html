Stop-word Removal

Removing extremely common words like "is", "the", and "and" that add little meaning to text analysis.

preprocessingtext cleaningfeature reduction
Prepared jointly by: Nupoor Mahajan & Hansa Gusaiwal
1 Definition

Stop-word removal is the process of filtering out common, low-information words - such as "is", "the", "and", "of" - from text before analysis.

2 Purpose

These words appear so frequently that they rarely help distinguish one document's topic or sentiment from another's. Removing them reduces noise and shrinks the vocabulary that later steps must handle.

3 Working Principle

A predefined stop-word list (available in libraries such as NLTK or spaCy) is compared against each token. Any token matching the list is discarded, and only meaningful "content words" remain.

4 Example
Worked example Input: "The movie was not good at all."
After stop-word removal: ["movie", "good"].
5 Advantages
  • Shrinks vocabulary size, speeding up later computations.
  • Focuses analysis on words that actually carry topical meaning.
  • Simple to apply using ready-made stop-word lists.
6 Limitations
  • Can remove important words for sentiment analysis, such as "not" or "never", changing the meaning entirely.
  • A generic stop-word list may not suit every domain (e.g. legal or medical text).
  • Not suitable for tasks that need full grammatical structure, like translation.
7 Applications
  • Topic modelling and document classification.
  • Search engine indexing.
  • Keyword extraction from student notes or reviews.
